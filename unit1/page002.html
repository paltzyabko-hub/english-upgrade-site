Reading Strategy #1

Skimming and scanning: read faster, find information, and answer with confidence.

🎥 Video Lesson

This video explains two important reading strategies:

  • Skimming — reading quickly to understand the main idea.
  • Scanning — looking for specific information such as names, dates or numbers.
Watch on YouTube Download PDF notes

📖 Video Notes

Learning goal: Understand the difference between skimming and scanning and know when to use each strategy.

Key Ideas

  1. Skimming means reading quickly to understand the main idea.
  2. Scanning means looking for specific information such as names, dates, numbers or keywords.
  3. Read the title and headings before reading the whole text.
  4. Do not translate every word. Focus on meaning.
  5. Return to the text when you need evidence for an answer.

Challenge for Mark

Before reading a new text, spend 30 seconds looking only at the title and headings.

Then write one sentence:

“I think this text is about...”
